# Safe Blog Wording - FDA Compliant GLP-1 Content

**Use these EXACT phrasings for blog articles**

## Semaglutide

### Weight Loss Data (SAFE)
✅ **"In STEP 1, semaglutide 2.4 mg produced a mean body weight reduction of 14.9% at 68 weeks in research participants."**

✅ **"Published clinical trial data from STEP 1 (NEJM 2021) demonstrated 14.9% mean weight reduction over 68 weeks with semaglutide 2.4 mg weekly."**

❌ **AVOID:** "Semaglutide causes 15% weight loss" (implies guaranteed outcome)

### FDA Approval (SAFE)
✅ **"Semaglutide received FDA approval for chronic weight management (brand name Wegovy, 2.4 mg weekly) on June 4, 2021."**

✅ **"Originally approved for type 2 diabetes in 2017 (Ozempic, up to 1 mg), semaglutide later received approval for weight management in 2021."**

### Mechanism (SAFE)
✅ **"Semaglutide is a GLP-1 receptor agonist. Published research indicates it delays gastric emptying and activates central appetite centers in the hypothalamus."**

✅ **"Preclinical studies demonstrate GLP-1 receptor activation contributes to satiety signaling through vagal afferent pathways."**

## Tirzepatide

### Weight Loss Data (SAFE)
✅ **"In SURMOUNT-1, tirzepatide 15 mg produced a mean body weight reduction of 20.9% at 72 weeks in research participants (NEJM 2022, Jastreboff et al.)."**

✅ **"Clinical trial data from the SURMOUNT-1 program demonstrated dose-dependent weight reduction: 15% (5 mg), 19.5% (10 mg), and 20.9% (15 mg) at 72 weeks."**

❌ **AVOID:** "Tirzepatide guarantees 21% weight loss"

### FDA Approval (SAFE)
✅ **"Tirzepatide received FDA approval under two brand names: Mounjaro (May 13, 2022, type 2 diabetes) and Zepbound (November 8, 2023, chronic weight management)."**

✅ **"First approved for diabetes in 2022, tirzepatide later received weight management approval in 2023."**

### Mechanism (SAFE)
✅ **"Tirzepatide is a dual GIP and GLP-1 receptor agonist, representing the first 'twincretin' approach combining both incretin pathways."**

✅ **"Research suggests the dual GIP/GLP-1 mechanism may contribute to appetite regulation through complementary central and peripheral pathways."**

### Comparative Claims (SAFE - Head-to-Head Only)
✅ **"In SURMOUNT-5 (NEJM 2025), a head-to-head comparison trial, tirzepatide produced greater weight reduction than semaglutide in participants with obesity."**

✅ **"Direct comparative research (SURMOUNT-5) demonstrated superior weight outcomes with tirzepatide vs semaglutide."**

❌ **AVOID:** "SURMOUNT-1 showed tirzepatide beats semaglutide" (cross-trial comparison, not head-to-head)

## Retatrutide

### Weight Loss Data (SAFE)
✅ **"In a Phase 2 dose-ranging trial (NEJM 2023, Jastreboff et al.), retatrutide 12 mg produced a mean body weight reduction of 24.2% at 48 weeks."**

✅ **"Published Phase 2 research demonstrated dose-dependent weight reduction: 17.5% (8 mg) and 24.2% (12 mg) at 48 weeks, representing the highest magnitude observed in GLP-1 class trials to date."**

❌ **AVOID:** "TRIUMPH trial showed 24.2%" (TRIUMPH is Phase 3; 24.2% is from Phase 2)

### Development Status (SAFE)
✅ **"Retatrutide is currently investigational, undergoing Phase 3 clinical trials under the TRIUMPH development program."**

✅ **"NOT FDA approved. Retatrutide is in Phase 3 trials (TRIUMPH-1, TRIUMPH-2, TRIUMPH-3) with expected data in 2025-2026."**

### Mechanism (SAFE)
✅ **"Retatrutide is a triple receptor agonist targeting GIP, GLP-1, and glucagon receptors—the first compound in this class to reach late-stage clinical development."**

✅ **"The addition of glucagon receptor activation may contribute to enhanced energy expenditure and hepatic fat oxidation in preclinical models."**

## General RUO Framing (Always Include)

### Standard Disclaimers
✅ **"This information is for research reference only and does not constitute medical advice."**

✅ **"Clinical trial data reflects outcomes in controlled research settings and does not predict individual responses."**

✅ **"Research Use Only. NOT for human consumption or therapeutic use."**

### Safe Research Language
✅ Use: "Published research indicates..."  
✅ Use: "Clinical trials have demonstrated..."  
✅ Use: "In research cohorts..."  
✅ Use: "Preclinical studies suggest..."

❌ AVOID: "You will lose..."  
❌ AVOID: "This causes..."  
❌ AVOID: "Take this for..."  
❌ AVOID: "Results in..."

## Citations (Blog-Friendly Format)

You don't need full academic citations for blog articles, but reference trial names:

✅ **"(STEP 1, NEJM 2021)"**  
✅ **"(SURMOUNT-1, Jastreboff et al., NEJM 2022)"**  
✅ **"(Phase 2 trial, NEJM 2023)"**  
✅ **"(SURMOUNT-5, NEJM 2025)"**

## FDA Compliance Checklist

Before publishing any GLP-1 content, verify:

- [ ] All weight loss claims cite specific trial names (STEP, SURMOUNT, Phase 2)
- [ ] No implied guarantees of outcomes ("causes" → "demonstrated in trials")
- [ ] Retatrutide clearly marked as investigational/not approved
- [ ] Tirzepatide FDA timeline includes BOTH approvals (2022 diabetes, 2023 obesity)
- [ ] Comparative claims use SURMOUNT-5 head-to-head data (not cross-trial)
- [ ] Prominent RUO disclaimers at top and bottom of article
- [ ] "Hunger noise" or similar colloquial terms framed as appetite regulation mechanisms

## Example: Safe Full Paragraph

✅ **Safe FDA-Compliant Version:**

> "In published clinical trials, GLP-1 receptor agonists have demonstrated significant weight reduction in research cohorts. Semaglutide 2.4 mg produced a mean 14.9% body weight reduction at 68 weeks in STEP 1 (NEJM 2021), while the dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist tirzepatide achieved 20.9% mean reduction at 72 weeks in SURMOUNT-1 (NEJM 2022). In head-to-head comparison (SURMOUNT-5, NEJM 2025), tirzepatide demonstrated superior weight outcomes versus semaglutide. Retatrutide, a triple agonist currently in Phase 3 trials, showed 24.2% mean reduction at 48 weeks in Phase 2 research (NEJM 2023). **Research Use Only—NOT for human therapeutic use. Clinical trial results do not predict individual outcomes.**"
