Why batch-specific testing matters

Manufacturing variance is normal

Even reputable synthesis labs produce batch-to-batch variance. Purity can shift 2-5% between production runs. Peptide degradation begins the moment synthesis completes. Without independent testing, you're trusting manufacturer self-reporting — an industry-wide reliability gap documented in third-party testing audits.

Untested batches carry unknown risks

Contamination with synthesis byproducts, incorrect peptide sequences, mislabeled concentrations, or bacterial endotoxin presence. Any of these compromise research validity. Vendor-supplied COAs are often recycled across batches or refer to "representative samples" tested months prior.

Chapter 005

Frequently asked questions

What does Janoshik-verified mean?+

Every batch is sent to Janoshik Analytical for independent third-party testing. This includes HPLC-DAD (purity), LC-MS/MS (identity), and endotoxin screening.

How do I verify my COA after receiving an order?+

Enter your VX Batch ID (printed on the vial label) into our Verification Portal. The full analytical report displays instantly with one-click Janoshik.com verification.

What happens if a batch tests below 99% purity?+

We enforce a minimum 99.0% HPLC purity threshold. Any batch testing below this is rejected and returned to the supplier. Only batches meeting our specification are released.

Are these products for human consumption?+

No. All Vantix Bio compounds are sold strictly for in vitro and in vivo laboratory research purposes only under the Research Use Only (RUO) exemption.

When does Vantix Bio officially launch?+

We are launching on May 1, 2026. Analytical testing is currently in progress at Janoshik Analytical for our initial 12-product catalog.

Why Janoshik Analytical?+

Janoshik Analytical is the peptide research community's verification standard due to publicly verifiable Task IDs, specialized peptide testing expertise, and 10+ years of track record.
