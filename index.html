Why Batch-Specific Testing Matters

Generic claims about "98% purity" mean nothing without batch verification. Here's why every batch gets independently tested.

Manufacturing Variance Is Normal

Even under GMP conditions, peptide synthesis produces batch-to-batch variance of 2-5% in final purity. Temperature fluctuations during lyophilization, reagent lot variations, and coupling efficiency differences create measurable purity drift — normal in peptide chemistry, but unacceptable without verification.

Real Example: Batch A tests at 99.1% purity. Batch B from the same manufacturer, same specification, tests at 97.8%. Both labeled "≥98%" — only independent testing reveals the 1.3% difference that affects experimental reproducibility.

What Untested Batches Risk

  • Failed experiments: Low-purity batches produce inconsistent dose-response curves, wasting months of research time.
  • Non-reproducible results: Published protocols fail to replicate when using different batches with undisclosed purity variance.
  • Contamination risks: Endotoxin presence (bacterial contamination) remains undetected without LAL testing, skewing immune-response research.
