When you're buying research peptides, you've probably seen vendors advertise "third-party tested" or "lab verified" products. But here's what most don't tell you: they test one vial from one batch and slap that COA on every vial they sell—sometimes for months.

That's not verification. That's marketing.

At Vantix Bio, we do something different: **batch-level testing**. Every time we receive a new batch from our supplier, we test it independently. New supplier shipment = new Janoshik COA. No recycling. No assumptions.

Here's why that matters.

## What Is a Batch?

A batch is a production run from your supplier. Think of it like this:

- **Batch 1:** 100 vials of Retatrutide 20mg shipped in March
- **Batch 2:** 100 vials of Retatrutide 20mg shipped in April

Same product. Same supplier. But different production runs = different batches.

Each batch can (and often does) have slightly different purity, identity, or endotoxin levels. Manufacturing isn't perfectly consistent—even from reputable suppliers.

## How Most Vendors Test

Here's the standard industry practice:

1. Vendor orders 500 vials of Tirzepatide
2. They send **one vial** to Janoshik for testing
3. They get a COA showing 98.5% purity
4. They sell all 500 vials using that **same COA**
5. Six months later, they reorder and do it again

**The problem:** What if vial #1 was 98.5% pure, but vials #200-300 were only 92% pure? What if the supplier changed something mid-batch? What if endotoxin contamination happened later in production?

You'd never know. You're trusting that **one sample** represents **500 vials** across **six months** of sales.

## What to Look For on a COA

When you're comparing vendors, here's what to check:

### 1. **Batch Number**
Does the COA show a specific batch number? Or does it just say "Tirzepatide 10mg"?

**Good:** "Sample ID: VX-TIRZ30-001"  
**Bad:** "Sample ID: Tirzepatide from ABC Labs"

### 2. **Test Date**
Is the COA recent? Or is it from 2023 and being recycled?

**Good:** Tested within the last 1-3 months  
**Bad:** Tested 6+ months ago, still being used

### 3. **What Was Tested**
Does the COA show:
- **HPLC purity** (what % is the actual peptide)
- **LC-MS identity** (is it the right peptide)
- **Endotoxin screening** (is it contaminated)

**Good:** All three  
**Bad:** Just purity (missing identity and endotoxin)

### 4. **Who Submitted It**
Some vendors claim "third-party tested" but the supplier submitted the sample—not the vendor.

That's not independent verification. That's the supplier grading their own homework.

**Good:** "Submitted by: Vantix Bio"  
**Bad:** "Submitted by: [Chinese supplier name]"
