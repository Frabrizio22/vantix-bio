What is Bacteriostatic Water?

Bacteriostatic water (BAC water) is sterile, non-pyrogenic water for injection containing 0.9% benzyl alcohol (w/v) as a bacteriostatic preservative. This precisely formulated solution serves as the standard reconstitution vehicle for lyophilized peptides and proteins in laboratory research settings. The benzyl alcohol preservative inhibits bacterial growth through disruption of microbial cell membrane integrity, enabling multiple sterile withdrawals from the same vial over an extended period—a critical advantage over sterile water for injection, which must be discarded after a single use.

The 0.9% benzyl alcohol concentration represents the optimal balance: sufficient to maintain bacteriostasis throughout the vial's usable life, yet low enough to avoid interference with most biological assays and peptide stability. This concentration has been validated across decades of pharmaceutical and research use as safe for peptide reconstitution, maintaining the structural integrity and biological activity of reconstituted compounds while preventing microbial contamination that would otherwise compromise experimental results.

Applications in Research

Peptide Reconstitution

The primary application for bacteriostatic water is reconstituting lyophilized (freeze-dried) peptides for laboratory research. The process involves adding a measured volume of BAC water to the peptide vial using a sterile syringe, allowing the peptide to dissolve without agitation. The benzyl alcohol preservative then maintains solution sterility through subsequent withdrawals, enabling researchers to use a single reconstituted vial across multiple experimental timepoints.

Proper Reconstitution Technique

  • Step 1: Swab the BAC water vial stopper and the peptide vial stopper with 70% isopropyl alcohol
  • Step 2: Using a sterile syringe with needle, withdraw the desired volume of BAC water
  • Step 3: Insert the needle through the peptide vial stopper, directing the water stream down the inside wall of the vial—never directly onto the lyophilized powder
  • Step 4: Allow the peptide to dissolve naturally over 1-3 minutes. Gentle swirling is described as preferred in published methods—do not shake or vortex
  • Step 5: Once fully dissolved, the solution should be clear and free of visible particles

Laboratory Dilution

Beyond peptide reconstitution, bacteriostatic water serves as a general-purpose preserved diluent for creating working solutions, serial dilutions, and standardized concentrations of research compounds. Its bacteriostatic properties make it suitable for multi-day experimental protocols where solution sterility must be maintained without single-use waste.

Compatibility

Compatible with virtually all research peptides at standard reconstitution concentrations. The 0.9% benzyl alcohol concentration does not significantly affect peptide stability or activity at typical use dilutions. Note: some researchers prefer sterile water without preservative for extremely sensitive cell-based assays where even trace benzyl alcohol could be a variable—in such cases, use single-use sterile water for injection.

Volume Calculations and Best Practices

Proper volume calculation ensures accurate peptide concentrations throughout your research program. For a typical peptide vial containing 5-10mg, adding 1mL of bacteriostatic water creates a working concentration suitable for most in vitro and in vivo research protocols. The relationship is straightforward: concentration (mg/mL) equals peptide mass (mg) divided by volume of bacteriostatic water added (mL). For example, adding 2mL to a 10mg peptide vial produces a 5mg/mL stock solution from which further dilutions can be prepared.

When withdrawing bacteriostatic water from the source vial, always use a new sterile needle for each peptide vial reconstitution to prevent cross-contamination between compounds. Insert the needle at a slight angle through the rubber stopper to minimize coring—where small fragments of rubber are inadvertently punched into the solution. If coring occurs, the particulate matter may not be visible to the naked eye but can compromise both solution sterility and experimental results. The 30mL format's larger stopper surface area accommodates more insertion points before stopper integrity is compromised, representing another practical advantage over smaller vial formats for active research programs handling multiple peptides.

For researchers managing multiple reconstituted peptide vials simultaneously, published literature suggests labeling each vial with the reconstitution date, volume added, resulting concentration, and expiration date (28 days from reconstitution). This systematic approach prevents confusion between similar-appearing vials and ensures peptides are used within their validated stability window. The 30mL format typically serves an active research program for 2-4 weeks, aligning well with the reconstituted peptide stability period and minimizing waste from unused bacteriostatic water expiring before the vials it reconstituted.

Storage & Handling

Store at controlled room temperature (20-25°C / 68-77°F). Do not freeze—freezing may compromise the vial seal integrity. Protect from direct sunlight. Do not use if the solution appears cloudy, contains visible particulate matter, or if the vial seal appears compromised. Once opened (first needle puncture), use within 28 days. Inspect the rubber stopper before each use—if coring or damage is visible, discard the vial.

The 30ml volume is sufficient to reconstitute approximately 15-30 peptide vials at standard 1-2 mL reconstitution volumes, providing excellent value for active research programs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between bacteriostatic water and sterile water?

Bacteriostatic water contains 0.9% benzyl alcohol that inhibits microbial growth, allowing multiple sterile withdrawals from the same vial over up to 28 days. Sterile water for injection contains no preservative and must be used immediately after opening—it cannot be re-entered.

Can bacteriostatic water be used for all peptide reconstitution?

Yes, BAC water is compatible with virtually all research peptides. The 0.9% benzyl alcohol does not affect peptide stability or bioactivity at standard reconstitution volumes. For extremely sensitive cell-based assays, some researchers prefer preservative-free sterile water to eliminate benzyl alcohol as a potential variable.

How many peptide vials can one 30ml vial reconstitute?

At typical reconstitution volumes of 1-2 mL per peptide vial, a single 30ml BAC water vial can reconstitute 15-30 peptide vials—making it sufficient for most multi-peptide research protocols.

How long is bacteriostatic water good after first use?

Once the vial is first punctured, use within 28 days. The benzyl alcohol preservative maintains bacteriostasis throughout this period, but beyond 28 days, accumulated microbial exposure from repeated needle entries may exceed the preservative's capacity.

Why is the 30ml format better than 10ml?

The 30ml format provides 3x the volume at significantly less than 3x the cost, reducing per-milliliter expense. It also reduces the number of vial changes during multi-peptide reconstitution sessions, minimizing potential contamination events.

Should bacteriostatic water be refrigerated?

No—store at room temperature (20-25°C). Refrigeration is unnecessary and freezing should be actively avoided as it may compromise the vial seal. After reconstituting a peptide, the reconstituted peptide solution should be refrigerated, but the BAC water source vial stays at room temperature.
