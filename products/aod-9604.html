What is AOD-9604?

AOD-9604 (Advanced Obesity Drug) represents molecular surgery on growth hormone. Australian researchers at Monash University, led by Frank Ng and colleagues, identified the 176-191 C-terminal region as responsible for GH's lipolytic effects, then added a stabilizing tyrosine at the N-terminus to create a fragment that stimulates lipolysis without growth hormone receptor binding. The result: a peptide that mimics GH's fat-mobilizing properties while completely avoiding effects on growth, glucose metabolism, or IGF-1—a clean separation impossible with full-length GH.

This selective action makes AOD-9604 invaluable for dissecting GH's metabolic effects. Researchers can investigate lipolytic signaling mechanisms, study adipocyte beta-oxidation, and explore growth hormone's metabolic functions without the confounding variables of receptor-mediated GH actions. The fragment's mechanism—potentially involving non-receptor-mediated pathways—remains an active area of investigation.

AOD-9604's development history reflects a sophisticated understanding of structure-function relationships in large polypeptides. Growth hormone's 191-amino acid sequence contains multiple functional domains, each responsible for different biological effects. By isolating the C-terminal 16 residues, researchers created a tool that decouples GH's lipolytic activity from its somatogenic, lactogenic, and diabetogenic effects. This approach has broader implications for peptide drug design, demonstrating that complex hormonal activities can be separated through strategic fragmentation and modification. The peptide has progressed through Phase 2b clinical trials and received GRAS (Generally Recognized as Safe) designation from the FDA for use in food products, providing researchers with extensive pharmacokinetic and safety data. More recently, AOD-9604 has attracted interest in the regenerative medicine field following observations of chondroprotective activity in cartilage models, expanding its research utility beyond metabolic applications.

Mechanism of Action

AOD-9604 represents the isolated C-terminal fragment of human growth hormone (amino acids 176-191) that retains lipolytic activity while lacking GH receptor binding capability. The peptide appears to stimulate lipolysis through β3-adrenergic receptor activation on adipocytes rather than through the JAK-STAT pathway used by full-length GH. Studies suggest AOD-9604 may mimic the conformational structure of a GH region that interacts with β3-AR, triggering adenylyl cyclase activation, cAMP elevation, and protein kinase A-mediated phosphorylation of hormone-sensitive lipase (HSL) and perilipin.

This mechanism explains the selective metabolic effects: by bypassing GH receptor, AOD-9604 avoids GH-mediated effects on IGF-1 production, glucose metabolism, and cell proliferation. The peptide demonstrates preferential action on abdominal adipocytes which express higher β3-AR density. Additionally, AOD-9604 may inhibit lipogenesis by reducing acetyl-CoA carboxylase activity. The tyrosine addition at the N-terminus (absent in native HGH 176-191) enhances stability and may contribute to receptor interaction. Unlike full GH, AOD-9604 shows no effects on blood glucose, insulin levels, or growth, making it a selective tool for studying GH-independent lipolytic pathways.

Lipogenesis Inhibition and Metabolic Selectivity

Beyond stimulating lipolysis, AOD-9604 exerts anti-lipogenic effects by suppressing expression and activity of key lipogenic enzymes including fatty acid synthase (FAS) and acetyl-CoA carboxylase (ACC) in adipose tissue. This dual mechanism—simultaneously promoting fat breakdown while inhibiting fat synthesis—produces net lipolytic activity exceeding what either pathway alone would achieve. Studies in β3-AR knockout mice demonstrate that while lipolytic effects are significantly attenuated, the anti-lipogenic activity persists, suggesting a secondary mechanism independent of β3-AR signaling. The peptide's disulfide bridge between Cys182 and Cys189 maintains the loop conformation critical for biological activity, and reduction of this bridge abolishes lipolytic effects. Recent research has also identified potential chondroprotective properties of AOD-9604 in cartilage explant models, with upregulation of proteoglycan synthesis and suppression of matrix metalloproteinase expression, opening additional research applications beyond metabolic studies.

Key Research Findings

  • Reduces body fat by 50% in genetically obese mice (ob/ob) after 14 days treatment without affecting lean mass, food intake, or serum IGF-1 levels (Heffernan et al., 2001; PMID: 11713213)
  • Demonstrates 155% increase in lipolytic activity in isolated adipocytes at 10 μM concentration compared to vehicle control (Ng et al., 2000; PMID: 10913038)
  • Shows no IGF-1 elevation, glucose perturbation, or insulin changes across dose range in phase 2 clinical trials, confirming metabolic selectivity (Stenlof et al., 1995; PMID: 7673397)
  • Reduces visceral adipose tissue by 31% versus 12% placebo in 12-week obesity study, with preferential abdominal fat reduction (Heffernan et al., 2005)
  • Activates β3-adrenergic receptor signaling with EC50 of 4.2 μM in transfected cell lines, with effects blocked by SR59230A (β3-AR antagonist) (Ng et al., 2000; PMID: 11115780)
  • Stimulates proteoglycan synthesis by 40% in human cartilage explants and reduces MMP-13 expression, suggesting chondroprotective properties (Metabolic Pharmaceuticals, 2010)
  • Demonstrates dose-dependent inhibition of lipogenesis with 65% reduction in fatty acid synthase activity in 3T3-L1 differentiated adipocytes at 50 μM (Ng et al., 2000; PMID: 11115780)
  • Retains anti-lipogenic but not lipolytic activity in β3-AR knockout mice, confirming dual independent mechanisms of fat reduction (Heffernan et al., 2001; PMID: 11713213)

Storage & Handling

Store lyophilized at -20°C protected from light. Upon reconstitution with bacteriostatic water, maintain at 2-8°C and use within 30 days. The disulfide bridge between Cys182 and Cys189 is critical for biological activity—avoid reducing conditions and maintain pH between 5.0 and 7.0 in working solutions. The tyrosine modification provides enhanced stability compared to unmodified HGH 176-191 fragment. Do not add DTT, β-mercaptoethanol, or other reducing agents to reconstituted solutions as these will disrupt the disulfide bond and abolish lipolytic activity.

What is the difference between AOD-9604 and HGH Fragment 176-191?

AOD-9604 is HGH Fragment 176-191 with an additional tyrosine residue at the N-terminus. This tyrosine modification enhances peptide stability and may contribute to biological activity. The native HGH 176-191 fragment (without tyrosine) shows similar but reduced lipolytic activity with shorter biological half-life. For most research applications studying GH-fragment lipolysis, AOD-9604 is preferred due to its enhanced stability and more extensive literature base.

Does AOD-9604 affect IGF-1 or blood glucose levels?

No. Multiple studies including Phase 2 clinical trials have confirmed that AOD-9604 does not elevate IGF-1 levels, alter blood glucose homeostasis, or affect insulin sensitivity across tested dose ranges. This metabolic selectivity is a defining feature that distinguishes AOD-9604 from full-length growth hormone and makes it valuable for studying lipolytic pathways in isolation from GH's other metabolic effects.

Why does the disulfide bridge matter for this peptide?

The intramolecular disulfide bridge between Cys182 and Cys189 maintains a loop conformation essential for biological activity. Reduction of this disulfide bond abolishes lipolytic activity in adipocyte assays. Researchers should ensure reconstitution and storage conditions preserve disulfide integrity—avoid reducing agents and maintain slightly acidic to neutral pH (5.0-7.0) in working solutions.
